## Tab 1 · Budget & Plan
### Quarterly Marketing Plan
Budget Request
- **Regional PIC**: Tim, Ruru
- **Process Steps**: 1. Regional sends notification in the 2nd month of the previous quarter, attaching Plan Template and Budget Sheet link with submission deadline
2. Local team uses Template to write quarterly marketing plan (strategy overview, KPI summary, details for Peak Day / SOL / CCP / Esports campaigns)
3. Local team adds a new tab in the Budget Sheet, filling in monthly amounts, budget logic, and KPIs for each line item
4. Submit by the first half of the 3rd month of the previous quarter
5. Regional reviews and provides feedback or approval
6. Regional consolidates all markets and submits HQ S&M budget request in the second half of the 3rd month of the previous quarter
- **What to Prepare**: Marketing Plan PPT (use Regional Template):
Strategy overview (incl. context analysis), KPI summary, budget overview, detailed campaign plans — must include: Peak Day Campaigns, SOL DAU Enhancement, CCP, eSports

Budget Sheet (add new tab): Each item must include:
Monthly breakdown / Total / Budget request reason & logic / KPI

Note: Markets that do not apply for S&M budget only need to submit the Marketing Plan PPT
- **Timeline**: Start drafting: 2nd month of previous quarter
Submit by: first half of 3rd month of previous quarter
- **Common Q&A**: Q: Can the plan template content (new patch/season info) be shared externally?
A: No. New season content and Esports schedules are highly confidential (strict need-to-know). Never share with anyone outside the authorized list.

Q: Does every budget item need a KPI?
A: Yes. Each item must include budget request reason & logic + corresponding KPI — required for HQ submission. Missing this will result in a revision request.
- **Related Links**: Budget Sheet / Plan Template (provided by Regional via email each quarter)

- **Regional PIC**: Ruru

① DAU data: A1 DAU on Peak Day (incl. WoW%, new users, PEAK CCU, no_play_30d) + A7 (same day of Peak Day week)

③ Top campaigns worth sharing (max 5)
④ Executive Summary: 1–2 sentences on market conditions, KPI vs target, biggest highlight, and #1 learning for the next Peak Day

Section 2 – Per Campaign:
① Objective ② What we've done (timeline, # KOLs, budget, channels) ③ Performance vs benchmark (KOLs / Content / Budget / Views / CPM/CPV) ④ Key Highlights ⑤ Learnings ⑥ Optimization Plan for next Peak Day
- **Timeline**: Start immediately after Peak Day ends → submit by Friday of the same week
- **Common Q&A**: Q: Should the report cover all campaigns?
A: All overall exposure data should be included. For campaign insights, focus on key campaigns and actionable learnings useful for future planning.

## Tab 3 · Assets
### Non-IP Social Media Asset Review (Operator Appearance)
- **Regional PIC**: Tim
- **Process Steps**: 1. Local fills in the Out-of-game Asset Review Google Sheet (columns B–H) and uploads assets to the designated folder
2. Regional (Tim) consolidates daily and submits to TC
3. TC feedback is translated and updated in column N
4. Regional sends email notifications to all markets twice daily: 11:00–12:00 and 18:00–19:00 GMT+8
5. Assets can only be published after approval
- **What to Prepare**: Column B – Region: Applicant's market
Column C – Attach the pic: Attach image directly (not a folder link)
Column D – What's the asset for: Asset purpose (Engagement / Esports / Local event / Others)
Column E – Usage: Specific use description
Column F – Assets link: Paste direct image link — not a folder link
Column G – release date: Planned publish date
Column H – note your mail: Responsible person's email for direct notification reply
- **Timeline**: TC review takes ~1 business day, but back-and-forth revisions may occur — submit at least 3 business days before the planned publish date
- **Common Q&A**: Q: Which assets require review?
A: Any asset that modifies an operator's appearance: costume changes, fan art comics, Chibi-style illustrations, memes with operator elements. When in doubt, always submit. Plain text translation does not require review.

Q: Most common reasons for TC rejection?
A: ① Too AI-generated in appearance (TC is sensitive to obvious AI art — if AI tools are used, refine the output so it doesn't look AI-generated); ② Compositing real human faces with operators (prohibited); ③ Content involving political, religious, sexual, or gambling themes; ④ Competitor references or elements inconsistent with brand identity.

Q: If an approved asset is only resized or has translated copy, does it need re-review?
A: No, but localized copy must be sent back to Regional for collection.

Q: Can we tell users the nationality of the operators?
A: No, we can not include "nationality" and "nation flags" in all posting.
- **Related Links**: [DF] Non IP asset review

### In-game Asset Review (All In-game Visuals — No Exceptions)
- **Regional PIC**: Mye
- **Process Steps**: 1. Local fills in the In-game Asset Review Google Sheet (columns B–I) and uploads assets to the designated folder
2. Regional (Mye) consolidates daily and submits to TC
3. TC feedback (with Chinese translation) is updated in column K
4. Regional sends email notifications to all markets twice daily: 11:00–12:00 and 18:00–19:00 GMT+8
5. Assets can only go live after approval
- **What to Prepare**: Column B – Region: Applicant's market
Column C – What's the asset for: Asset type (Ingame Banner / Popup / Entrance / Background / Radio, etc.)
Column D – Usage: Usage category (Esports / Local event / Activeness / KOL event, etc.)
Column E – Assets link: Paste direct image link — not a folder link
Column F – Attach the pic: Attach asset preview image
Column G – release date: Planned go-live date
Column H – note your mail: Responsible person's email
Column I – Activity description (Chinese): Brief event background for TC's reference
- **Timeline**: Submit at least 3 business days before go-live date
If assets are revised, re-submission requires another full review cycle (same-day two-batch turnaround)
- **Common Q&A**: Q: Do all in-game assets require review, even minor revisions?
A: Yes — no exceptions for in-game assets. All must be submitted for review.

Q: Most common reasons for TC rejection?
A: ① Too AI-generated (TC is increasingly sensitive — refine until it doesn't look AI-made); ② Design elements inconsistent with Delta Force style (e.g., rounded corners clash with the game's sharp UI — use triangular or angular lines); ③ Delta Force logo repeated too many times in-game; ④ Enemy NPC holding a player's weapon skin (creates player confusion).

Q: How quickly will I receive feedback?
A: TC reviews daily; feedback is emailed at 11:00–12:00 and 18:00–19:00 GMT+8. For urgent items, contact Mye directly on Seatalk.
